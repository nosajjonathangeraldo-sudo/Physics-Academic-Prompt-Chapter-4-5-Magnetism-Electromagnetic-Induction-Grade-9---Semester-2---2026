Dynamic Electricity

Electric Potential

Electric potential is the amount of energy each unit of charge has in an electric field. It is measured in volts, where 1 volt equals 1 joule per coulomb.

Electric Current

Electric current refers to how fast electric charge flows through a conductor. It is measured in amperes, meaning one coulomb of charge passes a point every second.

Ohm’s Law

This law explains the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance. The voltage across a conductor is equal to the current multiplied by the resistance.

V = I × R

Kirchhoff’s Laws

Kirchhoff’s Current Law states that the total current entering a junction is equal to the total current leaving it. Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law explains that the total voltage around a closed loop adds up to zero.

Series and Parallel Circuits

In a series circuit, the same current flows through all components. In a parallel circuit, the voltage remains the same across each branch.

Electromotive Force (EMF)

EMF is the energy supplied by a source, such as a battery, to move charge through a circuit, even if no current is flowing.

Electric Sources

Electrical energy can come from different sources such as batteries, generators, and solar panels, each converting energy into electricity in its own way.

Resistors

Resistors are components used to limit or control the flow of current in a circuit. They are also useful for dividing voltage and adjusting signals.
